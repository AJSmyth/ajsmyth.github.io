Featured Project

I've included some snippets of my favorite (publically sharable) work. At UCI I was electrical lead for the CanSat team. For this competition we designed a landing probe which was required to keep a raw egg safe during launch, descent, and impact while transmitting telemetry data throughout. Our design utilized a multi-stage heat shield which decreased the probe's speed before deploying a parachute. We collected and transmitted telemetry data in real time, consisting of accelerometer, gyroscope, airspeed (via pitot tube), GPS, barometric pressure, battery charge, current draw, and more. All systems and telemetry performed perfectly during launch day, aside from the camera. Due to intermittent overcurrent issues, we ultimately decided to disable it just before launch.

PCB Design

The PCB was designed using Altium. The microcontroller we used was a STM32F103, due to the prevalence of cheap Chinese development boards with this variant. It was also perfectly sufficient for our needs. We integrated several sensors in order to meet competition requirements. All boards were hand soldered. We developed requirements during meetings with the operations, mechanical, and software subteams before designing the PCB.Component selection was performed after developing power and link budgets to ensure compliance with our requirements.

I wanted our design to follow an iterative design process. We first constructed a breadboarded circuit to give the software team something to work with. The first PCB revision simply connected breakout boards for each respective sensor to an STM32 BluePill. This PCB met all of our requirements aside from power consumption and weight, allowing the software and mechanical teams to make progress and perform basic integration testing early on. Due to these stringent weight an power consumption requirements, we developed a second revision with switching converters, less breakout boards, and proper keepouts for optimized RF performance.
